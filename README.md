This repository documents **Encuentro Nacional 4/20²⁶ Pro-Legalización 🌿** as a **replicable cultural celebration of free entry and free participation**.

It is rooted in Bolivia, with **Proyecto Cultural Barranco** as a documented reference case, but it is designed to be useful beyond a single space, city, or edition. It is also open to future translations and adaptations for other territories, languages, and legal frameworks, if enough interest and stewardship emerge. The goal is not only to announce an event, but to build an open structure that other host spaces, collaborators, and communities can understand, adapt, improve, and potentially replicate across other cities and territories.

## Core idea

The 4/20 encounter shows that a spontaneous, diverse, collaborative, and careful community is possible. **Voluntariado Barranco** seeks to cultivate that same possibility throughout the year: a freer, more responsible, and more human way of living, organizing, and creating together.

We believe that open, well-cared-for, and increasingly visible cultural celebrations can help reduce stigma, invite skeptics and the general public into a more mature conversation, and gradually bring Bolivia closer to a serious discussion about cannabis legalization. The main strategy is not confrontation, but social visibility through example, hospitality, culture, and shared experience. Replicating 4/20 events across different cities is not a minor logistical detail: it is part of the movement’s strategy. Every space that opens helps make the community more visible, reduce stigma, and turn legalization into a conversation that becomes harder to ignore in public life.

## How people can participate

The project is open to many kinds of participation, including:

- Host spaces
- Low-risk support or diffusion points
- Artists and musicians
- Visual artists and exhibitions
- Panelists and public conversations
- Ventures / emprendimientos
- Virtual events and streams
- Other categories that make sense for the encounter, even if they were not anticipated in advance

Not every space or collaborator needs to participate in the same way. Part of the model is precisely that different people and places can join with different levels of visibility, commitment, and risk.

## 2026 timing and electoral context

- **Main date:** Monday, April 20, 2026.
- **Optional activities:** Friday April 17 through Sunday April 19, depending on each host space and local context.
- **Proyecto Cultural Barranco** is treating April 18–20 as its reference case in La Paz.

In **La Paz, Cochabamba, and Potosí**, the April 17–20 logic can be sustained more naturally depending on the space.

In **Oruro, Beni, Chuquisaca, Tarija, and Santa Cruz**, the runoff election on Sunday, April 19 strongly conditions any public activity that weekend. Election day itself should not be treated as a usable public-programming date, and in those departments the focus should be placed especially on Monday the 20th or on very carefully designed formats.

## What is being launched first

The first active call focused on **host spaces**, but the main layers of participation are now already public: host spaces, petition, artists and music, visual artists / exhibition, public conversation, ventures, and virtual participation.

The encounter also aims to treat participation more fairly and transparently than many cultural calls do. If the event generates revenue — especially through the bar or other public-facing activity — the intention is first to cover real operating needs, including exceptional transport or special logistics for artists or other key participants when needed, before any remaining surplus is considered for the broader **Voluntariado Barranco** fund. Public accountability and transparent reporting are part of the model.

The WhatsApp layer is also already active, with a general chat, department-based groups, and thematic support groups around the Manual 4/20, legal orientation, and medicinal orientation.
